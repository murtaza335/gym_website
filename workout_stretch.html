Side Lunges

Side lunges, also known as lateral lunges, are one of my favorite bodyweight leg exercises of all time. And though they may seem pretty simple at first, they're actually pretty difficult for a lot of people because they require a lot of leg strength as well as flexibility and balance in order to do them properly.

Calf Stretch

The ends of the gastroc and soleus tendons fuse at the ankle to create what we know as the Achilles tendon. Stretching these muscles will prevent injury to the ankle and the many little muscles that help stabilize the ankle and the possibility of tearing your Achilles.

Glute Bridge

It works the hamstrings, lower back, abs, in addition to the glutes. With many of the benefits similar to that of a squat, another plus for the glute bridge is that it does not place any pressure on the lower back. This is also a great exercise for people who are unable to squat due to back, hip, or knee pain.
